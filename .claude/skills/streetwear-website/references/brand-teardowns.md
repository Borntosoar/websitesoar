# Brand teardowns — the most profitable streetwear brands

Per-brand website/DTC analysis. **Private-brand revenue figures are estimates.**
Sources listed at the bottom. Last researched: 2026-06.

---

## 1. Supreme — the archetype
- **Scale:** ~**$538M** revenue (FY ended March 2024). Acquired by
  **EssilorLuxottica for $1.5B** (2024), after VF Corp bought it for **$2.1B**
  (2020). [1][2][3]
- **Release model:** new product **every Thursday ~11:00 ET**, in-store + online,
  in deliberately limited quantities; items sell out in **~19–173 seconds**,
  feeding a massive resale market (StockX/Grailed). [4][5]
- **Website factors to steal:**
  - **Radical minimalism** — plain ground, tiny nav, box logo, single product
    photo, terse copy. The *drop*, not the design, is the product.
  - **Ritual cadence** — the audience organizes its week around the drop.
  - **Honest sellouts** as social proof; scarcity is real, not faked.
  - Fast, no-nonsense checkout built to survive spike traffic.

## 2. Stüssy — the original, still winning by restraint
- **Scale:** ~**$155M** total sales (2025 est.); **stussy.com online ~$135M**
  (2024 est.). Privately, family-owned; famously "never sold out" the brand. [6][7]
- **Website factors:** OG surf-skate restraint; rolling limited drops without
  Supreme's clock; consistent house typography (the hand-style logo as system);
  global but curated. Lesson: **longevity via scarcity + independence**.

## 3. Kith — editorial + retail theater on Shopify Plus
- **Scale:** ~**$135M** revenue (2025 est.); built on **Shopify Plus**. [8][9]
- **Website factors to steal:**
  - **Editorial storytelling** — lookbooks, "chapters", brand films; emotion +
    identity woven around product. Content *is* the merchandising.
  - **Monthly program** cadence (the "Kith Monday Program") = predictable drops.
  - Retail-as-experience (Kith Treats) reflected in a premium, image-led site.
  - Robust **loyalty / membership**; strong email capture.

## 4. BAPE (A Bathing Ape) — the signature-graphic system
- **Scale:** private; owned by **I.T Group** (Hong Kong, acquired 2011). Decades-
  long global icon, especially strong in Asia. *(revenue = estimate, low conf.)* [10]
- **Website factors:** dense **graphic merchandising**; the **camo + ape-head**
  as relentless brand furniture; regional storefronts; collab-led homepages.
  Lesson: a **proprietary motif applied everywhere** is itself the luxury signal.

## 5. Off-White — luxury-street, LVMH-backed
- **Scale:** **LVMH took a majority stake (2021)**; founded by Virgil Abloh.
  *(revenue = estimate, private within LVMH.)* [11]
- **Website factors:** **industrial design language** — quotation marks
  ("SCULPTURE"), caution-tape, zip-tie, cross-arrows, Helvetica caps; higher AOV
  presented with editorial/runway imagery; **collaboration as headline**. Lesson:
  a strong conceptual motif maps street energy onto luxury price points.

## The next wave (high website value for a new brand)

- **Palace** — London; Supreme-style **weekly Friday drop**; irreverent, web-
  native humor; lookbooks shot like skate videos. Independent. *(est.)*
- **Aimé Leon Dore** — cinematic **editorial lookbooks**, "quiet luxury" street,
  NYC heritage storytelling; Shopify; imagery-first PDPs. Founded 2014. *(est.)* [12]
- **Fear of God / Essentials** — minimalist neutral system; **Essentials** is the
  high-volume diffusion line (accessible price, huge reach); restrained type. *(est.)*
- **Corteiz (Crtz)** — Clint419; **guerrilla drops**, password/coming-soon gates,
  deliberate friction & exclusivity, geo stunts; community-first. Exploded
  2023–24. Lesson: **manufactured friction can raise desirability**. *(est.)*

## Cross-brand pattern summary

| Factor | Minimalist camp | Graphic camp | Editorial camp |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|----------------|
| Exemplars | Supreme, Stüssy, Palace, Essentials | BAPE, Off-White | Kith, Aimé Leon Dore |
| Homepage hero | The drop / product grid | Collab motif | Cinematic lookbook |
| Copy | Near-zero | Slogan/motif | Narrative |
| Photography | Product on plain ground | Graphic/product dense | On-location, on-model |
| Conversion driver | Scarcity + ritual | Identity + collab | Lifestyle aspiration |

All three camps share: **drop scarcity, mobile-first fast checkout, email/SMS
capture, sold-out honesty, and one decisive CTA.**
